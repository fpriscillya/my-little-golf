Before you film

Good footage gives good feedback. The app needs to see you from head to feet, wrists included, throughout the entire swing.

Angle 1 — Face-on

Camera directly in front of you, at hip height, perpendicular to your target line. Reveals weight shift, hip turn and posture.

Angle 2 — Down-the-line

Camera behind you, pointing toward your target, at hand height. Reveals swing plane, spine angle and club path.

  • 01 Film in landscape mode. Portrait crops your feet out of frame.
  • 02 Use slow-motion if your phone supports it, 120 fps or 240 fps. Impact lasts 0.2 seconds.
  • 03 Distance: 3 to 4 metres from the golfer. Your full body, feet to wrists, must stay in frame throughout the swing.
  • 04 Film outdoors or in good light. Shadows behind you cause tracking errors.
  • 05 Filming alone: set your phone on a tripod, a bag, or a water bottle. Use your phone timer delay.
  • 06 With a friend: ask them to keep the phone steady and film through to the finish. Do not stop at impact.
