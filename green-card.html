Theory and rules

Pass your Green Card

The Pass Carte Verte is your passport to play on French courses. It is not a test of how far you hit the ball. It proves you know the rules, the etiquette, and how to play safely without slowing others down. Here is what to know, and a quiz to check yourself.

What the Green Card actually is

An official FFGolf attestation of your autonomy on a course. Four things worth knowing before you start.

It validates behaviour, not performance

It certifies you can play an 18-hole course safely and at a reasonable pace, without disturbing other players. Your savoir-etre, not your handicap.

It lasts for life

Once obtained, the Pass Carte Verte never expires. Unlike your FFGolf licence, you do not renew it each year.

You need a current FFGolf licence

The current-year FFGolf licence is required to sit the exam. Without it, the instructor cannot validate your pass.

It is your ticket onto real courses

Most French courses ask for it, or an equivalent, before letting you play 18 holes. It is the simplest proof you can play without creating danger.

What is tested

The pass is built on a 3-level scale of minimum skills. The exam usually combines a short theory check with an on-course situation.

1

Safety

Never swing or hit when someone could be in range. Know where everyone is. A course is not a driving range, people can be out of your sight line.

2

Pace of play

Keep up with the group ahead. A slow group blocks the whole course behind it. Be ready to play when it is your turn, and let faster groups through.

3

Rules and etiquette

Basic rules: lost ball, out of bounds, unplayable, penalty areas, simple drops, marking on the green. Etiquette: repair pitch marks, rake bunkers, stay silent and still while others play.

How to get it, step by step

  • 1Take out the current-year FFGolf licence (around 62 euros). This is mandatory.
  • 2Choose a path: regular lessons, an intensive stage, or, if you already have the level, go straight to the exam.
  • 3Study the rules and etiquette. The theory check is usually done first because it is quick.
  • 4Pass the on-course situation: play safe forward shots, keep a good pace, apply the rules. Direction matters more than distance.
  • 5The instructor validates your pass with FFGolf. You receive confirmation in your Espace licencie.
